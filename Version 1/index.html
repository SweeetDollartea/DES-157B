What is an REE and why does it matter?

A Rare Earth Elements, represented in the periodic table, is a group of 17 metallic elements, also known as rare earth oxides or lanthanides. They are significant components of devices we use in our everyday lives. These include smart phones, eletric cars, batteries, and wind turbines. They are essentially the bottom of the supply chain for Apple, Samsung, Tesla and even more. They are called "rare", but there is actually an abundance of these elements, only rare to extract them.
