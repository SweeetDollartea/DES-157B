Take an iphone for example

Inside are major components of an Iphone, in which Rare Earth Elements (REE) are used. These include, but not limited to the MagSafe, Taptic Engine and the Microphone.

A Rare Earth Elements, represented in the periodic table, is a group of 17 metallic elements, also known as rare earth oxides or lanthanides. They are called "rare", but there is actually an abundance of these elements, only rare to extract them.

They are significant components of devices we use in our everyday lives. These include smart phones, electric cars, batteries, and wind turbines. They are essentially the bottom of the supply chain for Apple, Samsung, Tesla and even more.

The mining conditions of these REE mines are dangerous. Workers are often exposed to dust and chemicals that can lead to respiratory issues, cancer and long-term health effects. Extracting these elements also creates radioactive wastes, in which if they are not handled correctly, it can lead to environmental and water contamination.
